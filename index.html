Abstract

Recent advances in the field of autonomous mobile robotics have enabled their deployment in a wide spectrum of environments ranging from congested city streets and complex warehouses to hazardous areas such as underground mines and burning buildings. A majority of this advancement has relied on using large amounts of manually labelled data to train various state-of-the-art deep learning algorithms. However, manual annotation is prohibitively expensive for large amounts of data which hinders model scalability and prevents the deployment of models in a wide range of environments where annotated data is not readily available.


The goal of this workshop is to analyse different paradigms in the field of robotics that can be leveraged to train models with limited supervision. Specifically, this workshop shall explore various works in the fields of self-/ weakly-supervised learning, K-shot learning, and transfer learning among others. Further, this workshop shall also investigate the use of rich feature representations generated by many emergent foundation models such as DINO, CLIP, and GPT4 to reduce or replace manual data annotation in the existing training protocols. Through this workshop, we aim to provide a platform for researchers from various disciplines such as robotics, computer vision, representation learning, and deep learning to exchange ideas and promote collaborations. This workshop also aims to propel research in this exciting direction that would enable the large-scale deployment of autonomous mobile robotics in various facets of our day-to-day life.

Call for Papers


The goal of this workshop is to create a forum that enables the free exchange of ideas between experts from various communities such as robotics, computer vision, and representation among others. As a result, we invite both early-career as well as experienced researchers to submit work focusing on, but not limited to, the following topics:


  • Self-Supervised, Weakly-Supervised and Unsupervised Learning
  • Zero- and K-Shot Learning
  • Representation Learning
  • Leveraging Foundation Models for Data-Efficient Learning
  • Transfer Learning
  • Knowledge Distillation
  • Domain Adaptation
  • Multi-Modal Distillation
  • Continual Learning
  • Incremental Learning
  • Open World Learning
  • Active Learning

We invite you to submit high-quality research either as a 2-page extended abstract or a 4-page short paper. Page counts exclude references (i.e., 2 + n and 4 + n). You are encouraged to use ICRA's suggested paper template and upload a PDF. The review process will be single blind, that is, the authors' names are not required to be anonymized, aligned with ICRA paper submissions. We encourage submissions of work-in-progress and work that is not yet published. Accepted papers will be presented as posters during ICRA2024 in Yokohama, Japan, with a selected few in the spotlight lightning session.
